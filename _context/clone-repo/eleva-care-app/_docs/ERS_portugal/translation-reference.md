# ERS Legal Document Translation Reference Guide

_Last Updated: December 2024_

This reference guide documents the canonical ERS-aligned phrases across all legal documents (EN, ES, PT, PT-BR) for auditing and maintaining translation consistency.

---

## Purpose

- Ensure all translations faithfully reflect the Portuguese-law anchored legal position
- Prevent jurisdiction drift or colloquial adaptations
- Maintain ERS compliance across all language versions

---

## Core Principles

1. **Portuguese Law Anchor**: All documents are governed by Portuguese law
2. **No Jurisdiction Drift**: Do not reference local laws (Brazil, Spain) for clinical matters
3. **Parallel Structure**: Maintain identical sentence structure for platform vs. expert responsibilities
4. **No Marketplace Language**: Use "digital health platform", not "marketplace"
5. **Language Precedence**: Portuguese version prevails in case of divergence

## Final QA Checklist

When auditing translations, verify:

- [ ] **Definitions**: Block exists and terms match across all languages
- [ ] **Platform Description**: "digital health platform" (not "marketplace")
- [ ] **Telemedicine Reference**: "(teleconsultations / telemedicine)" appears once in platform description
- [ ] **Non-Interference**: Exact phrasing about clinical judgment preserved
- [ ] **Organizational Responsibility**: "organizational, technical, and administrative responsibility" present
- [ ] **Client–Expert Relationship**: Healthcare relationship is "directly" between client and expert
- [ ] **Liability**: Parallel structure for platform vs. expert responsibilities
- [ ] **Professional Registration**: References "applicable law" or "Portuguese law" (not local Brazilian/Spanish law)
- [ ] **Governing Law**: Explicitly states Portuguese law governs
- [ ] **Language Precedence**: Disclaimer present in footer (PT-BR specifies "português de Portugal")
- [ ] **No Drift**: No "laws of your country apply", no old "marketplace" disclaimers, no implied clinical oversight
